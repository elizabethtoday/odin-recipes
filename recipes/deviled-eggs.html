Red White and Blue Deviled Eggs

Description

Try this perfect 4th of July appetizer! Deviled eggs are a tried and true barbeque staple, so why not bring it on this for this year's gathering? This is a great way to bring some holiday festivities all the way to the table. Start with the perfect deviled eggs, and then dip them in food coloring to take it up a notch.

We will also add a little kick to the traditional simple recipe by adding a bit of horseradish! The flavor kick with the eye-catching decor makes these a win at the potluck or family back yard bbq.

Ingredients

  • 3 drops red gel food coloring
  • 2/3 cup water, or as needed
    • 2 drops blue gel food coloring
  • Toothpicks
  • 8 Hard Cooked Eggs (peeled)
  • 21/2 tablespoons mayo
  • 1 tablespoon hot prepared horseradish
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1 pinch fresh ground black pepper
  • 3/4 teaspoon smoked hot paprika
  • 1 teaspoon snipped fresh chives (optional)

Steps

  1. Combine red food coloring and 1/3 cup water in a ramekin; do the same with the blue food coloring in a separate ramekin.
  2. Insert 3-4 tootpicks into eggs and balance it so that one end is dipped into one of the dyes
  3. Slice colored eggs in half lengthwise. put the yolks into a bowl
  4. Mix mayo, horseradish, rice vinagar, salt, pepper and the yolks into a bowl and stir until smooth. Transfer filling into a piping bag with a large start tip.
  5. Pipe filling into each egg white half. Sprinkle deviled eggs with chives. Chill until ready to serve.
